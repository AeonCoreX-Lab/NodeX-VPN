Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 29, 2026 · Effective immediately

The short version: NodeX VPN collects no logs, no usage data, and no personal information about your VPN activity. The only data we handle is your Google account email — stored encrypted on your own device, never on our servers.

1. Who We Are

NodeX VPN is a product developed by AeonCoreX. We build open-source privacy tools. Contact us at aeoncorexbd@gmail.com.

2. What We Do NOT Collect

We structurally cannot collect the following because NodeX VPN uses the Tor network — relays we do not own or operate:

  • VPN traffic content or metadata
  • Websites or services you visit while connected
  • Your IP address during VPN sessions
  • Connection timestamps, duration, or frequency
  • Bandwidth or data usage
  • Device identifiers, hardware fingerprints
  • Location data
  • Browsing history

This is not a policy promise — it is a technical fact. Even if compelled by a court order, we have no VPN usage data to provide.

3. What We Do Collect

Google Account Email (CLI & Desktop only): When you use nodex login or sign in via the desktop app, your Google account email address and display name are stored locally on your device using the OS keychain. This is used only to display your account name in the interface. It is never transmitted to any server operated by AeonCoreX.

OAuth2 Token: A refresh token from Google is stored encrypted in your OS keychain (macOS Keychain, Windows Credential Manager, Linux Secret Service, or ~/.nodex/credentials.json with mode 0600 as a fallback). This token is never sent to AeonCoreX servers.

Mobile Apps: The Android, iOS, tvOS, and Android TV apps use Firebase Authentication managed by Google. We receive only the user's email and UID from Firebase. No VPN activity is logged.

4. Google Authentication

We use Google OAuth2 for authentication. When you sign in, your authentication request is processed by Google under Google's Privacy Policy. We receive only your email address and display name. We never receive your Google password.

5. Firebase (Mobile Apps)

Our mobile apps use Firebase Authentication (by Google) for user management. Firebase may collect device and usage analytics per its own privacy policy. We do not use Firebase Analytics or Crashlytics — only Authentication.

6. GitHub

Source code, releases, and issues are hosted on GitHub. GitHub's privacy policy applies to interactions with our repository. Download statistics visible through GitHub are aggregate counts — individual users cannot be identified.

7. npm Registry

The CLI is distributed via npm as @aeoncorex/nodex-vpn. npm may collect aggregate download statistics. We cannot identify individual users from npm statistics.

8. Third-Party Tor Relays

Your traffic is routed through Tor relays operated by independent volunteers worldwide. These relays are not operated by AeonCoreX. Each relay only sees one hop of your circuit — no single relay can identify both the origin and the destination of your traffic. The exit relay can see the destination but not your identity.

For destinations that use HTTPS (the vast majority of the modern web), the exit relay also cannot read your traffic content.

9. Children's Privacy

NodeX VPN is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact us and we will delete it.

10. Data Retention

We retain no server-side user data. Credentials stored on your device can be deleted at any time by running nodex logout (CLI) or signing out in the app. Deleting the app removes all locally stored data.

11. Changes to This Policy

If our practices change, we will update this policy and the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of NodeX VPN after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

12. Contact

Questions or concerns about this privacy policy: aeoncorexbd@gmail.com
