// How it works

Three hops between you and the internet.

NodeX wraps every TCP connection in three layers of encryption and routes it through three independent Tor relays. No single relay knows both who you are and what you're accessing.

💻
Your Device
🛡️
Guard Relay
🔄
Middle Relay
🌐
Exit Relay
🔒
Destination
01

Guard Relay

Knows your IP but not your destination. NodeX uses a trusted, stable guard for your long-term anonymity.

02

Middle Relay

Knows neither origin nor destination — just passes encrypted traffic forward. Prevents correlation attacks.

03

Exit Relay

Knows the destination but not your identity. You can choose the exit country for geo-specific access.

nodex login

Authenticate with your Google account. Opens a browser window for OAuth2 sign-in. Credentials saved securely to OS keychain.

nodex login
# Opens browser → Google Sign-In ✔ Logged in as user@gmail.com

nodex logout

Remove saved credentials from OS keychain and disconnect any active session.

nodex logout ✔ Logged out successfully.

nodex whoami

Show the currently authenticated account and token status.

nodex whoami Email: user@gmail.com Name: Your Name ✔ Token valid

nodex connect

Connect to the VPN. Routes all TCP traffic through a Tor circuit. Shows live bootstrap progress and real-time stats.

# Auto exit country nodex connect
# Specific country nodex connect --country DE
# Bridge mode (for censored networks) nodex connect --bridge "obfs4 1.2.3.4:443 FINGERPRINT cert=... iat-mode=0"
# Custom SOCKS5 bind address nodex connect --socks 0.0.0.0:9050
# Verbose logging nodex connect --verbose
OptionDefaultDescription
--country ISOautomaticExit node country (US, DE, NL, JP…)
--bridge LINEnoneobfs4 bridge line. Repeatable.
--socks ADDR127.0.0.1:9050SOCKS5 listen address
--dns ADDR127.0.0.1:5353DNS-over-Tor listen address

--verbose / -voffDebug logging
--quietoffNo banner, pipe-friendly

nodex status

Show current connection status, exit IP, throughput, and circuit info.

nodex status
Status : ✔ Connected Exit country: DE Exit IP : 185.220.x.x Uptime : 12m 34s ↑ Upload : 2.14 MB/s ↓ Download : 4.87 MB/s Latency : 198 ms Circuits : 3 active 1 pending

nodex nodes

List available VPN exit nodes with latency and load information.

nodex nodes nodex nodes --country NL nodex nodes --bridges

nodex logs

Show recent engine log output. Useful for debugging connection issues.

nodex logs nodex logs --lines 100

nodex version

Show full version info including build target and date.

nodex version

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026. Effective immediately.

The short version: NodeX VPN collects no logs, no usage data, and no personal information about your VPN activity. The only data we handle is your Google account email (for authentication) stored encrypted on your device.

1. Who We Are

NodeX VPN is a product developed by AeonCoreX ("we", "us", "our"). We build open-source privacy tools. Our contact email is aeoncorexbd@gmail.com.

2. What We Do NOT Collect

  • VPN traffic content or metadata
  • Websites you visit while connected
  • Your IP address (VPN sessions)
  • Connection timestamps or duration
  • Bandwidth usage
  • Device identifiers
  • Location data

This is structurally enforced: NodeX VPN uses the Tor network — volunteer-operated relays that we do not own or control. We have no technical ability to log your VPN traffic even if compelled.

3. What We Collect

Google Account Email: When you use nodex login or sign in via the desktop app, we store your Google account email address locally on your device using the OS keychain. This is used only to display your account name and is never transmitted to our servers.

Google OAuth2 Token: A refresh token is stored encrypted in your OS keychain (macOS Keychain, Windows Credential Manager, Linux Secret Service, or ~/.nodex/credentials.json with mode 600). We do not receive or store this token on any server.

4. Google Authentication

We use Google OAuth2 for authentication. When you sign in, Google processes your authentication request under Google's Privacy Policy. We only receive your email address and display name from Google. We do not receive your Google password.

5. GitHub

Our source code, releases, and issue tracker are hosted on GitHub. GitHub's privacy policy applies to interactions with our GitHub repository.

6. npm Registry

NodeX VPN CLI is distributed via the npm registry (@aeoncorex/nodex-vpn). Download statistics visible to npm are aggregate counts — we cannot identify individual users from these statistics.

7. Third-Party Tor Relays

Your traffic is routed through Tor relays operated by volunteers worldwide. These relays are independent and not operated by AeonCoreX. Each relay has its own privacy characteristics. The Tor network is designed so that no single relay can identify both the origin and destination of traffic.

8. Children's Privacy

NodeX VPN is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

9. Changes to This Policy

We will update this policy if our practices change. The "Last updated" date at the top of this document will reflect any changes. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.

10. Contact
